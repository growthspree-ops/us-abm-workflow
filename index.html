ICP & Tier Definition
One-time setup · Reviewed quarterly · Every account that enters the workflow is bucketed here first
Before any outreach begins, every target company is assigned to one of three tiers based on how well it matches your ICP and how much intent it's showing. This tier determines how much human effort and spend is allocated to the account — Tier 1 gets full-touch treatment, Tier 3 gets automation only. Getting this right upfront stops SDRs from wasting effort on low-fit accounts and ensures your best accounts get the attention they deserve.

💡 How to think about this
🎯 Why three tiers?
With 2–5 SDRs covering 500–1,000 accounts monthly, you can't give every account the same level of effort. Tier buckets enforce intentional resource allocation — high effort where it matters most.
📐 What defines Tier 1?
Strong ICP fit plus an active intent signal — not just company size. A $500M firm with no intent signals is Tier 2. A 200-person firm actively hiring a RevOps lead and visiting your pricing page is Tier 1.
🔄 Tiers are dynamic
Accounts move between tiers as their score changes. A Tier 3 account that attends your webinar can jump to Tier 2 or 1 automatically via HubSpot workflow rules.
⚡ Key Principle
80% of SDR bandwidth should be spent on accounts scoring 60+. The score enforces this — it's not a suggestion, it's the queue order.

💡 How to think about this
🤖 Why AI scoring vs manual?
Manual scoring is done once and forgotten. AI scoring re-evaluates every account weekly, catches new hiring signals, updates tier assignments automatically, and surfaces accounts that have moved into a buying window since last week.

Warming Phase — Pre-Outreach Only
2–3 weeks for Tier 1 · 1–2 weeks for Tier 2 · Outreach is unlocked only after minimum warming threshold met
The single most common ABM mistake is going straight to outreach on cold accounts. This phase runs before a single email or LinkedIn DM is sent. Buying committee members at your target accounts are shown a 3-stage sequence of LinkedIn ads — building name recognition first, then relevance, then a soft nudge. When the SDR's connection request finally lands, it's not cold. The prospect has already seen your content multiple times.

💡 How to think about this
🔥 Why warm before outreach?
Cold outreach to an unwarmed account has a 1–3% reply rate. The same message to an account that's already seen 3 weeks of your content gets 2–4x higher reply rates. The warming phase is what makes the outreach phase work.
📢 TOFU → MOFU → BOFU logic
You don't lead with a pitch. Week 1 is pure education — you're not even asking for anything. Week 2 shows you understand their world. Week 3 introduces a very light CTA. By the time the SDR reaches out, they've seen your thinking 3 times already.
📊 Impression cap — why it matters
3–5 impressions per account per week pushes account reach from the typical 10–15% of the buying committee to 80–90% — meaning most stakeholders in the account see your ad before anyone is contacted.
⚡ Output of this phase
Outreach sequences are unlocked per account — only after warming threshold is confirmed. HubSpot workflow enforces this automatically.
Warming threshold confirmed → multi-channel outreach sequences activated

Multi-Channel Outreach
3 channels coordinated · Staggered 3–5 days apart · No pitch before establishing context · Every reply logged in HubSpot and updates account score
Outreach runs across LinkedIn, email, and phone in a coordinated, staggered sequence — not simultaneously. The logic is simple: LinkedIn builds the human relationship, email provides context and a clear ask, and calling is reserved for accounts already showing strong engagement signals. Each channel knows what the others are doing. An SDR never leads with a call to a cold account. A connection request is never sent without a warming period behind it.

💡 How to think about this
🔀 Why coordinate channels?
A prospect who receives a LinkedIn connection, sees your ad, and gets an email referencing the same context experiences a coordinated brand touchpoint, not three random cold contacts. It feels like you've done your homework, not like a spray-and-pray campaign.
🎯 Signal-personalised opener
Email 1 is not a template — it's triggered by a specific signal: a new job posting, a funding round, a recent LinkedIn post, or a web visit. The hook comes from data, not from generic copy. This is what Clay + Claude Cowork enables at scale.
📞 Why call last, not first?
Cold calling without prior context has a <2% connect-to-conversation rate. Calling after 2 LinkedIn touches + 1 email boosts this to 8–12% because the prospect has already encountered you and may even be expecting the call.
⚡ Output of this phase
Every reply — positive, negative, or neutral — is logged in HubSpot and adds to the account score. Replies are the first buying signal from the account itself.
All outreach replies + engagement → HubSpot engagement score updated in real-time

💡 How to think about this
📋 The priority queue is the product
Everything in the workflow — the data collection, the scoring, the signal capture — exists to produce one thing: a ranked list of accounts that tells SDRs exactly who to contact today and in what order. This is the output that makes everything else worth building.
🔒 Why mandatory logging?
If SDRs don't log calls and meetings in HubSpot, the score is wrong. A wrong score means the priority queue is wrong. One person skipping logging breaks the system for the whole team. This must be a non-negotiable protocol enforced from day one.
🤝 SLAs replace manual management
Without SLAs, a hot account sits in the queue for 3 days because nobody noticed the score moved. SLAs turn the CRM into a self-managing system — the right person gets a task and a Slack ping the moment the threshold is crossed.
⚡ Output of this phase
Every account above 90 has an active HubSpot task assigned. No hot lead sits uncontacted. The priority queue runs the team's day — not intuition, not a spreadsheet.
